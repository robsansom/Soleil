# Soleil English Copy Tone

This note records the current direction for Soleil website and App Store copy. It is a refinement guide, not a rewrite mandate.

## Positioning

Soleil helps people make better everyday decisions in the sun by turning UV, SPF, clothing and daylight history into clear personal guidance.

## Voice

- Plain-spoken, precise and reassuring.
- Warm because it is useful, not because it is poetic.
- Specific before emotional.
- Confident about the product mechanics, careful about health outcomes.
- App Store-ready: the first sentence should say what the app does.

## Prefer

- Live UV, SPF, clothing, skin type, time remaining, reapply reminders, Apple Watch daylight, family check-ins, on-device privacy.
- "Estimate", "guidance", "helps", "tracks", "reminds", "shows".
- Concrete scenarios: starting a session, checking SPF, seeing daylight history, timing shade.

## Avoid

- Personifying the app as a friend, guardian or companion.
- Over-polished contrasts such as "not X, but Y" unless they carry precise information.
- Vague emotional claims such as "a kinder way" or "quietly scientific".
- Language that implies medical certainty or guaranteed burn prevention.
- Copy that sounds warm before it has earned trust through specifics.

## Working Rule

When a line feels too generated, make it more observable: what data does Soleil use, what action does it help with, and what should the user understand next?

## Localization

All locales (de, es, fr, ja) follow this guide, not just the English. When English copy changes, update the other locales in the same pass. Translations should carry the same plain-spoken register: no personification, no invented poetry, and product terms stay consistent per locale (e.g. Sun Window = "Schutzzeit" in German, "fenêtre de soleil" in French, "ventana de sol" in Spanish, "サンウィンドウ" in Japanese).
